      <td style="text-align:left;"><strong>Property Tax</strong></td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Immobile base; Promotes accountability; Stable yield.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Technically difficult to assess; Politically unpopular; Can be regressive if poorly administered.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Requires strong and transparent administrative capacity for property valuation, registration, and collection.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td style="text-align:left;"><strong>Business Licenses/Fees</strong></td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Relatively easy to administer; Can be linked to local services consumed by businesses.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Revenue potential may be limited; Can be arbitrary and discourage local economic activity if not well-designed.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Fee structure must be predictable, transparent, and non-punitive to foster a good business climate.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td style="text-align:left;"><strong>User Charges</strong><br><em>(e.g., for water, waste collection)</em></td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Links payment to consumption (efficient); Recovers costs for service provision.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Can be inequitable, harming poor users' access to essential services.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Requires carefully designed subsidies or lifeline tariffs to protect access for the poor.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td style="text-align:left;"><strong>Surcharges on National Tax</strong><br><em>(e.g., personal income)</em></td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Easy to administer ("piggybacking"); Revenue can grow with the economy; Broad base.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">Can reduce local autonomy and accountability if rates are not locally set; Can exacerbate regional inequalities.</td>
      <td style="text-align:left;">The degree of local discretion over setting the surcharge rate is the critical determinant of its value for decentralization.</td>
    </tr>
